Rules

Whether you're new to the game or need a little reminder, this list will aid you in your adventures through Wit Lib Land.


Adjective: a word that describes the subject. (itchy, blue, flawless, round, complicated, ect.)

Comparative: modifies an adjective with an 'er' suffix. (stronger, faster, louder, greater, lamer, ect.)

Superlative: modifies an adjective with an 'est' suffix. (wildest, best, farthest, prettiest, etc.)

Adverb: a word that is used to change, modify, or qualify other words. (always, carefully, hard, everywhere, well, etc.)

Noun: a person, animal, place, thing, or idea. (teacher, zebra, airport, piano, bravery, ect.)

Plural (pl.) Noun: indicates more than one. (women, sheep, oceans, potatoes, etc.)

Proper Noun: begins with a capital letter and names something that is one-of-a-kind. (Jupiter, Cleopatra, Oreos, The Simpsons, ect.)

Verb: an action word that describes what the subject is doing. (eat, run, endeavor, sleep, discern, mask, scamper, etc.)

Verb Present Participle/Gerund (ing): a verb ending with 'ing'. (falling, stomping, bellowing, stinking, ect.)

Verb Past-Tense (past): an action word that describes what the subject has already done. (flew, jumped, remarked, articulated, fortified, cobbled, etc.)
