The Three Little Pigs

Once upon a time there were three little pigs who lived with there mother in a small cottage. When the pigs were old enough, their mother bid them farewell to make their fortune in the world...

The first little pig came upon a wheat field where a farmer was gathering straw. "I shall make a fine hut with this.", said the pig, and asked the farmer kindly for several golden shocks...

Barely had the last straw been placed when a big bad wolf came to the door. "Little pig, little pig, let me come in!" the wolf demanded. "Not by the hair of my chinny chin chin." replied the pig, quaking. "Then I'll huff1 and I'll puff1 and I'll blow your hut down!" shouted the wolf, who blew with all his might. The straw was thrown asunder as the little pig fled over the hill in utter terror...

Meanwhile, the second little pig was strolling by a babbling brook where an old woman was tying bundles of ash staves. "These will make for a splendid house indeed.", proclaimed the pig, and asked the old lady kindly for a few sturdy bundles...

Hardly had the last stave been set when the big bad wolf came to the door. "Little pig, little pig, let me come in!" the wolf demanded. "Not by the hair of my chinny chin chin." replied the pig, shaking. "Then I'll huff2 and I'll puff2 and I'll blow your house down!" bellowed the wolf, who blew with all his might. The staves fell like match sticks as the little pig fled beyond the forest in abject horror...

Meanwhile, the third little pig was frolicking through a(n) flowery meadow where a brick maker and his son were loading many bricks onto a cart. "Those are just the ticket.", exclaimed the pig, and asked the fellows for a load of red bricks...

Scarcely had the last brick been mortared when the big bad wolf came to the door. "Little pig, little pig, let me come in!" the wolf demanded. "Not by the hair of my chinny chin chin." replied the pig, defiantly. "Then I'll huff3 and I'll puff3 and I'll blow your bungalow down!" howled the wolf, who blew with all his might... but nothing happened. The wolf tried again... still nothing. The big bad wolf tried 50 more time(s) but the bungalow would not fall. "You cannot blow down my bungalow, you silly old wolf, so begone at once!" shouted the pig. At these words, the wolf became furious and yelled, "fiddlesticks!", then stomped away into the distance never to be seen again.
