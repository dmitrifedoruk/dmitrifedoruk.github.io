Return to Thy Thistle Lord's Keep

Taking the Biscuit

By: DonnyWhoLovedBowling

Summary: Someone has stolen Remus’ chocolate! Will this old Marauder solve the mystery and have his revenge against the thief?

Notes: See end of story for notes.

It was early afternoon when Remus finally, and very much reluctantly, opened his eyes. He was lying face down in bed with his head resting halfway off his pillow, his arms flung out haphazardly from his body and his legs hopelessly tangled within the sheets. He attempted to kick them free without much success while trying to recall exactly what had happened since his return to human form.

Remus could vaguely remember Sirius helping him up from the cellar and into their room at sunrise, but not being placed in the bed. A delicate kiss on his brow and gentle touch of his cheek were followed by a whispered, “Sleep now, love.” His memories were always a bit muddled the morning after, just one of the many downsides to being a werewolf.

His head throbbed slightly as he sat up and looked about the room. The first thing he noticed was an onslaught of sickly greenish-grey light being cast from the window that faced the road. He sighed. Bloody weather. Remus had grown accustomed to the nearly constant state of rain and fog this spring, but he rather hoped April would turn around with a few good days before its end.

He loved the sunshine and the early blooming flowers and the gentle breezes this time of year. Remus huffed. A leisurely stroll through Regent’s Park would do wonders for his constitution, but apparition was out of the question in his fatigued state and Remus knew from the way he was shaking that he would be lucky if he made it half as far as Kings Cross on foot. What he really needed was a fix, but that would have to wait until he’d emptied his bladder.

He desperately needed to concentrate on pulling himself up out of bed. Remus did so with great effort and began to carefully stretch his overtired and aching muscles. Several places in his spine popped as he stood with his arms above his head, and his knees buckled when he took a few tentative steps. Still rather wobbly, Remus staggered into the bathroom to relieve himself and clean his teeth.

His jaw was terribly sensitive after a transformation, and a toothbrush was always so harsh against his gums, but he didn’t trust himself to perform even a simple Tergeo spell in his current condition. At least the minty freshness would help Remus to feel more human. Of course, this meant that he would need to stand in front of the basin, with that damned mirror just itching to taunt him. Maybe he would get lucky this time and it wouldn’t say anything.

Gazing at his reflection, Remus took in the unsightliness that was his usual post-transformation self. His face was pale and clammy, with a bristly stubbled chin, bloodshot eyes, and prematurely greying tousled hair. There hadn’t been any new cuts or scratches on his face in a long time, but the old and faded ones still retained a reddish tint that belied their age.

Turning on the tap, he splashed his face with cold water and ran a comb through his hair. It helped marginally but the haunted look in his eyes remained. All right. Let’s have it, then. Remus waited for the disembodied voice to start heckling him. When nothing happened, he proceeded to add a respectable amount of minty paste to his toothbrush. He almost had it in his mouth when the mirror’s voice pierced the silence, causing Remus to drop his brush into the basin.

“Oh, my!” The phantom said in that entirely haughty tone it reserved just for Remus. “Another bender! You really must learn to take better care of yourself.”

“Sod off.” Remus replied gruffly, reapplying fresh toothpaste to his brush.

“Perhaps you could seek counsel from your friends or join an addictions group.” The mirror quipped. “It might help you to admit to your little problem, you know.”

There was no point in responding any further to the bloody thing, it was the same every month. No, he hadn’t been drinking the night before. Yes, he understood just how terrible he must look. No , there wasn’t anything he could do about being a werewolf and would it please shut the hell up, once and for all.

Oh, how he wished he could remove it from the wall. Remus had tried to remove it, but the stupid thing simply laughed and taunted him further. Blasted sticking charms! Thank you very much for that ‘Noble and Most Ancient House of Black’.

Remus rinsed his mouth out and dried his face before turning towards the door once again. He was nearly through it when the mirror called out its parting shot to him.

“You need to stop all the boozing. You are far too decrepit for a wizard in his mid-sixties.”

“I’M FORTY-ONE, YOU BASTARD!!!”

Leaving the bathroom, Remus had to fight the urge to crawl back into bed and sleep for the rest of the afternoon. He sighed miserably. He didn’t know why he was complaining though, it could have been much worse. In fact, it had been far worse before he started taking the experimental Wolfsbane in January.

Severus had assured him that the side effects would be quite minimal, which they were with this most recent iteration. The addition of Mistletoe created a numbing effect during the transformation, and although it still hurt like hell, it was almost bearable. The powdered Tulsi leaves were also helping, and he was able to retain more memories now while in his wolf form. Three extra Nightshade berries, crushed, allowed him to rest contentedly during the long hours until dawn without the irrepressible urge to bolt through his cage and into the moonlit night.

Remus was eager to say the least and had leapt at the opportunity to try it. The trial, Severus had said, would be finished in June and he would be able to compile his data and send off for the patent if he was successful. Of course, Remus surmised that Severus had already perfected the variant, and knew that he must have worked tirelessly on the potion to get it this far. Severus never divulged as to why he was being so magnanimous, but the sentiment was nevertheless very much appreciated.

He suspected Albus had something to do with it, what with his penchant for meddling as he saw fit. That knowing twinkle in his eyes… Just what are you playing at, you old sod?

Remus was too exhausted to delve any deeper into the meaning behind such generosity. His energy was being diverted into steady breathing while trying not to wince as he began his descent down the three , very long flights of stairs towards the kitchen. The aches and sluggishness lessened with each step, but it would still be several more hours before his body would feel anywhere close to comfortable. Remus encouraged himself to push on with the constant reminder of getting his fix once he arrived at his destination.

He’d finally made it to the first-floor landing and nearly stumbled from the sudden, low muttering that was coming from behind a curtain covered portrait in front of him. Oh, Mrs Black! How could I have ever forgotten about you? Carefully, Remus tiptoed past Walberga sleeping in her frame and away from the potential catastrophe. That’s all he’d need in his condition, the daft old woman screaming the place down about half-breeds and filth. Sirius had tried time and again to remove his mother’s portrait, and with each attempt had only further succeeded in enraging the old crone, making her even louder and more obnoxious. Perhaps Remus would suggest some muggle turpentine on his next attempt.

He was grateful that they had managed to completely renovate the rest of the house. With exception to ‘Old Mumsie’ and that ruthless mirror, it was nearly impossible to tell that this was, in fact, The Grimmauld Place. All the claustrophobically Slytherin sentiments had been replaced with a modern, breezy open floor plan full of creams and lavenders. There were large comfy sofas and shag pile rugs soft as clouds underfoot, massive floor-length bay windows with delicate chiffon curtains, and pale oak recessed bookshelves full of everything but those dreadful dark art heirlooms that had no place in their home.

Once in the soft-lit kitchen, he managed to heat the kettle and warm the tea pot. With a generous amount of Darjeeling leaves added, he poured the hot water over, his hands trembling just a bit. Blast it all! Remus just couldn’t wait; he needed his fix now.

He glanced up at the shelf above the sink, the one with all the disused muggle items that Sirius just had to collect. An ode to the continued disruption of his parents’ pureblood ideology. There, behind the panini press and vacuum sealer was his tin. The one that was so old and tarnished you could only just make out the words ‘Keep Calm, …something, something’ written along the side. Remus wearily reached for his tin, taking care not to knock over the Dalek salt and pepper shakers in front of it, and pried it open eagerly to find….

Nothing.

What?

Nothing. Not a single sodding gram. Where the bloody hell is it? It might not have bothered him so much if it had only been a small chunk, but damn it all, it was an unopened bar! An entire, 480-gram bar, of Honeydukes newest chocolate marvel, The Bee’s Knees. He had bought it last week and was saving it for today, just in case he needed a little something extra after dinner.

None of this would have mattered if he’d just saved those Terry’s Chocolate Oranges and Chocolate Frogs on his bedside table the day before. He could have laid there for at least a little while until the tremors subsided, happily munching away at the sweets. But no, Remus had been so anxious that he even polished off the remains of his stash in the library as well as the one in the drawing room. He’d needed all of it just to keep himself calm as the moon approached last night. Even if there were any left, he was still too shaky to perform an Accio.

As if to add insult to injury, he’d just remembered the old writing desk in the study. There were some Chocolate Cauldrons shoved behind the bust of Cornelius Agrippa, but it was at least two floors up and his bone-weary body just couldn’t make the stairs again.

Feeling rather dejected, Remus fell back into his chair, no longer caring for his favourite cuppa. He really wanted his chocolate though. Nothing would ever compare to the euphoria he felt from indulging in a bar or two. Well, almost nothing, but he wasn’t up for any of that today. Still, he needed something to ease his discomfort. Then he remembered the canister filled with drinking chocolate that Molly had given him for Christmas, charmed with an ever-lasting spell. Bless that plucky little witch .

Remus immediately filled a large pot with a generous amount of cocoa and milk. He summoned all his strength to cast a basic heating charm, then reached for a large wooden spoon before returning to the vat. He inhaled deeply while stirring his beloved drink and watching the steam rise. Oh, how he loved the aroma of chocolate! Remus grabbed the largest mug he could find from the cupboard and filled it to the brim with the piping hot, frothy drink.

He resumed his seat and sipped at his cocoa, letting the velvety liquid course though his body while contemplating the mystery of his missing Bee’s Knees. This was just too bizarre for Remus. Who in their right mind would steal from him and how did they even know it was there to begin with?

He so wanted to try that bar, with its promise of decadent silkiness and crunchy honeycomb. It was probably sweeter than he would have preferred, but Remus just couldn’t help himself when it came to chocolate. He was an equal-opportunist and felt that cacao, in any form, deserved to grace his palate at least once.

Plus, it gave him a thrill every time he tried a new variety, especially the more exotic ones, and in his excitement, boundless questions would arise and plague his thoughts for hours on end. Was Mexican chocolate really ever too gritty? Should he choose Peruvian bean-to-bar over Ecuadorian? Could Hawaiian Criollo survive the Rose Beetle this year? Would a couple of trees flourish in the back garden? Would Sirius notice if he set up a conche in the larder? Would it work properly with magic, and if not, could he figure out how to rig it up with some kind of muggle wires? Have I completely lost the plot?

Another large, steaming mug full of chocolate was called for and then it would be back to the problem at hand.

Who did it? There were plenty of people coming and going to and from Grimmauld Place, but most of them never spent enough time there to notice his tin. The Order still had meetings there once a fortnight, although there was nothing decidedly worrying to talk about since the end of the war three years ago.

Maybe Dung took it, but no, he hadn’t been seen for more than a month, not since his last endeavor with those spurious cauldrons.

Fred and George were over last week, but that was the day before he’d bought the bar. Although, they could give the Marauders a run for their money when it came to mischief, so they were still contenders. How easy would it have been for them to sneak in during the wee hours of the morning?

No, Remus. You are daft if you think they’d come all the way over here for your sweets when they have plenty of their own in the shop. He was becoming paranoid in his weakened condition. He desperately needed that chocolate. Remus drained his mug, filled it to the brim once more and resumed his train of thought.

Harry couldn’t do it. He’d been living with them for the past two years during his Auror training and in all that time not once had he been even the slightest bit unscrupulous. However, some of Harry’s friends had been at the weekend for a knees up to celebrate his recent achievements at the Ministry. It hadn’t occurred to Remus that someone else might pilfer his tin, but perhaps that might have been due to his pre-transformation state of mind.

With the impending full moon in 3 days’ time, Remus had been uncomfortable in such a turbulent atmosphere for long. He’d left shortly after congratulating Harry, retreating to the bedroom with a good book, and charming his refuge into silence. His fatigue eventually got the better of him and Remus succumbed to slumber as the party wore on into the night. He always felt terribly guilty having to abandon social events in the name of his unyielding lycanthropy. Harry understood though, he was such a good lad. So kind, just like his mum.

So, Harry’s guests? Half of them he knew were friends, the other half were trainees of the same department or Ministry colleague well-wishers. Remus ran through the carousel of images in his mind, each person he’d seen that night, and how they behaved. Everyone was relaxed and carefree. Well, except for Neville, of course….

Oh, Merlin! I must be going barmy. That’s as bad as accusing Sirius! ….. Sirius? .... No, definitely not. He loved Remus with all his heart and would never abscond with his chocolates, not even for a laugh. There were things you just didn’t do to your partner of more than 20 years.

Remus returned his tin to the shelf, poured himself another large mug of cocoa and pondered further.

Logically, it really could be anyone, but somehow, he kept coming back to Sirius. Perhaps it was his addled, chocolate-deprived wits that had him drawing this conclusion, but his heightened wolf senses were telling him otherwise. He knew Sirius though, really knew him. He knew him when they had their first kiss in sixth year at Hogwarts. He knew him when the verdict came back guilty, and they were parted for twelve miserable years. He knew him when the Minister pardoned Sirius with an Order of Merlin, First Class and they wept openly. And he knew him now, that through all their little ups and downs, they would always be together no matter what happened. So, Sirius wouldn’t do it… he just wouldn’t. Would he?

Remus shook his head and drummed his fingers on the table. With a heavy sigh, he retrieved his tin once more and sniffed the lid. The unmistakable scent of Sirius was all over it. That could mean anything. Sirius did in fact know about the tin. Maybe he’d moved it aside in search of one of the many gadgets they kept on the shelf? Sirius truly had no reason to touch his tin otherwise. It belonged to Remus and readily contained one of his most valued possessions. He sniffed again. The scent was strong, as in recent.

A swirl of images assaulted Remus. Recently handled, recently pilfered, recently devoured! Sirius laughing madly while holding the Bee’s Knees above his head in triumph. Sprinting out of the room like a mad-man and bounding up the stairs to the attic. Ripping the paper to shreds in a frenzied attempt to get at its chocolate-y insides…

Remus felt dizzy. He was either over-dosing on sugar from his cocoa or going insane. He thumped his now empty mug down onto the table and rubbed a hand over his face.

Remus sat there for a long time, contemplating why Sirius would take his chocolate without asking and why he’d even want it in the first place. He preferred biscuits normally, and there was certainly no shortage of those in the house. Flapjacks, fingers, wafers, shortbreads, sandwiches… Sirius probably had every variety known to mankind pass through this kitchen at least once, especially the muggle ones. His most favourite of all were Raspberry Jammie Dodgers, and he took great pride in letting the empty packets clutter up the place terribly. Another jab at Mumsie’s impeccable taste.

Remus was overtly startled out of his revery by Harry slumping down into the chair opposite him. He looked completely knackered, with his hair sticking out even more at odds than it usually did. “Tough day?” Remus inquired kindly.

Harry shook his head. “Just long.” he replied, running his hand over his face. It was nearing dusk and Remus hadn’t even noticed. “Training since four this morning. Kingsley informed us that they’ve found a hideout in Lithuania and two in Denmark.” Harry leaned back in his chair and closed his eyes. “I think he wants us ready before autumn so we can help the local authorities shut them down. I can’t say I’m miffed though; I’ve been waiting to become an Auror for almost two whole years.” He sighed.

Remus chuckled. “You’d happily trapes around the world for the rest of your life if it meant keeping all of us safe, wouldn’t you?”

“Yeah.” Harry smiled. “Is there tea?” he asked as an afterthought, looking over at the kettle.

“Yes. But I’m sure it’s gone cold by now.” With a wave of his wand, Remus managed to elegantly make a new pot of tea and place it along with a cup in front of Harry.

“You’re feeling better then?” Harry noticed. “Using magic, I mean.”

“Yes. Well, I’ve had quite a bit of cocoa to help with my recovery.” Remus replied, tapping at the rim of his mug.

Harry cocked his head to the side and furrowed his brow. “I thought you usually went straight for a solid bar the day after?”

“Alas, there was no chocolate today.” Remus said glumly. He noticed the quick glance Harry gave to the shelf and just had to ask him. “How long have you known about that?” He said, inclining his head in the general direction of his tin.

Harry shifted in his seat, looking uncomfortable. “Well, a while I suppose.” He said, rubbing the back of his neck nervously. “Don’t be mad or anything, but Sirius told me about it. I’d noticed you always seemed to have a bar with you, especially after the full moon, but they’re never just lying around, so I asked him where you kept them. He said you were part magpie; told me you have little hiding places all over the house.” Harry added with a slight grin. “Sirius said that was one of your ‘little treasure troves’ up there and told me never to open it. He also said it was, um… cute.” Harry dropped his eyes to his cup before taking a sip.

“Did he?” Remus asked, narrowing his eyes. We’ll see who’s cute when I get my hands on…

“Please, don’t be mad at him. It really is sort of cute.” Remus frowned and Harry quickly clarified. “I mean, that he thinks that what you do is cute. You know… that even after all this time he can still find you so… interesting.” Harry’s cheeks were tinted pink as he stared into the bottom of his cup. “Not that you’re not, though. I just mean, well… I hope it’s like that for me and Ginny in 20 years.” He added with a smile that did not reach his eyes.

The image of blond hair and a pale, pointy face came to mind then, but Remus brushed aside that thought. There was no use contemplating that particular hypothetical as he attempted to bolster the young man’s confidence. “I’m sure it will be.” His answer being vague enough that Remus didn’t feel like he was outright lying to the cheerful boy. Even after everything that had happened to him in his short life, Harry was still so positive. And who knew, maybe he really would be just as happy then as he was now. No matter who his partner was, Remus hoped that Harry would be content for the rest of his life.

He sighed and continued. “No, I’m not mad at Sirius for telling you. He’s right of course, I can go a bit funny over chocolate. I absolutely love the stuff, and I can’t seem to live without it.” Remus chuckled. “Sirius sometimes refers to it as the other man in my life.” At this, Harry snorted and rolled his eyes.

“Cacao…” Remus mused, staring off into the distance. “It’s incredible how much of an impact it’s made on all of us.” Yes, an intoxicatingly powerful commodity. It was most precious to many Mesoamerican cultures. The Olmecs cultivated it. The Mayans revered it. The Aztecs valued it more than gold. Thousands of years of devotion. Then… European Colonialism… Remus shuttered. Such devastation. He was quiet for a moment, contemplating whether or not to go further into detail for Harry’s sake. No, best not depress and enrage him just this minute. Instead, Remus settled for, “Most people rarely ever think beyond their Curly Wurly…” Harry just stared back at him quizzically.

Remus cleared his throat. “Aside from the inferior sweets produced merely for profit by tasteless conglomerates, there are some truly amazing artisanal chocolates all over the world. Some people still do everything by hand in small batches,” he said in awe, “and there are even mail-order subscriptions to sample all sorts of these confections.” Remus looked excitedly at Harry who in turn was smirking strangely at him. He pushed on anyway.

“Did you know that in addition to dark and milk and white chocolate, there’s also red? It’s made from unfermented beans, and it has a decidedly fruity note to it. It’s not really talked about much because many people don’t actually consider it to be chocolate in the traditional sense. I thought the sample I had was wonderful. I even spoke with a fellow last summer about it, and he seemed rather eager to try it out himself. He said he was looking for a new idea to patent, which surely was not what I had intended when I brought it up, but there you have it…” Harry’s eyes were starting to glaze over.

“Have you ever tried cacao nibs? Most people think they’re too bitter, but I love the unparalleled earthiness of an unadulterated bean.” Remus continued enthusiastically. “Oh, did you know that every variety has its own unique flavor as well, and that even within a particular growing season or tree these flavors can change? It’s all so very fascinating.” You’re rambling, Remus.

Harry burst out laughing. “You sound just like Hermione.”

“Is that really such a bad thing?”

“She can be rather…”

“Brilliant? Inciteful?”

“Irritating.”

Remus scoffed.

Harry chuckled. “No offence mate, but I think my brain might start to melt if you try cramming it full of facts anymore this evening.”

Remus cleared his throat again. “Well. I was going to mention that cacao is highly nutritious and that it does wonders for both muggles and magical people, but… I suppose that’s enough waffling, eh?” Harry was smiling at him now in the way that James used to whenever Remus would go off on a tangent, and his heart ached from it. I miss you, you bloody Marauder.

Remus sighed. “It really does help, though. Not just to dispel a depressive state, as you might assume, but also with cognizance.” He spelled his mug to refill itself one last time. “I often feel quite addled without it.” he said before taking a coveted sip.

“Perhaps I am a bit possessive of my ‘little treasure trove’, but just knowing it’s there when I need it gives me strength.” Remus said, reinforcing his grip around the warm mug in his hands. “Not having any at my disposal usually makes me quite anxious and even a bit shirty at times, something I’m sure Sirius has most likely told you by now, if you haven’t noticed it yourself already.” Harry hummed in agreement.

Remus felt a bit sheepish at that. Don’t self-deprecate or you’ll ruin his evening with your gloominess. “Right now, I’m just grateful that Molly chose to gift me this.” he said with a little smile before putting the mug to his lips.

“Yeah, she’s amazing. I’d be lost without those smashing birthday cakes and Christmas jumpers.” Harry commented affectionately, before pouring himself another cup of tea. “So, you’re ok with just this, then?” He eyed the mug skeptically.

“It’s not nearly as potent, but I think it will suffice for now.” Remus assured him before sipping again. “Besides, I think I’ve had more than enough sugar for one day.”

“Even for Moony?” Harry chuckled.

“Yes. Even for Moony.”

“Well, I’m glad you’re all right.” Harry remarked, before sipping his tea. “So, what happened to your chocolate? Did you forget to restock your tin or something?”

“Oh, no.” Remus assured him. “I distinctly remember placing it in there. It was a new Honeydukes bar, The Bee’s Knees, and I was quite keen to try it.”

“But… where is it, then?” Harry hesitated, trying to decipher the mystery. “S-surely it wasn’t nicked?”

Remus sighed. “I believe it must have been.”

“Who would steal your chocolate, Remus?” Harry asked perplexedly.

“I think it might have been Sirius, for a laugh maybe.”

“Why would he do that to you? He knows how badly you need it after the full moon.” Harry thought for a minute, furrowing his brows intently. “Do you think it had anything to do with you tidying up after him last week?” he asked nervously.

Remus hummed. “I have no idea, but I plan to get to the bottom of it.” And when I do, he’s…

“You were a bit harsh on him.” Harry added gently.

Remus wished he’d had the foresight to cast a silencing charm during that row. “He’d made such a terrible mess of the library, Harry. There were books scattered everywhere! If I hadn’t stepped in and finished organizing for him, it would still be like that.” He remembered only too well how it had gone from there. Sirius was in a right strop when he came in and saw that Remus had put everything back in its place. He just went on and on about his vampire romance novels and how he’d worked so hard to finally categorize them by their best bits, as he called them. Before Remus could even apologize and reshelve the books for him, Sirius had stomped out in a huff. Yes, that was probably it.

Sensing Remus’ perturbance, Harry asked, “Is there anything I can do? Would you like me to pop down to the shops for something?”

“No thank you, Harry. As I’ve said, this is a rather copious amount of cocoa and I feel well enough to at least make it through the night.” Harry still didn’t look convinced. “I have a few Chocolate Cauldrons upstairs if I really need them later.” he added. Thinking about the Cauldrons led to dwelling on his missing Bee’s Knees and dwelling on his missing Bee’s Knees reminded him of Sirius’ transgression. He needed to think of a cunning, Marauder-esque plan for getting back at him, something that Sirius would not soon forget. Harry was staring at him, Remus realized, and quickly changed the subject. “So, are you off to visit Ginny this evening?”

Harry’s face lit up. “Yeah, she’ll be at The Burrow after nine. Gwenog wanted them to put in a couple of extra hours at practice, otherwise she’d be at the mercy of Molly’s forth helpings by now.” Harry chuckled, then sipped his tea again. “Ginny said they’ve been working on a last-minute offense against the Wasps. I can’t wait to see what they can do tomorrow afternoon. Oh, that reminds me-” Harry fished in his robe pocket. “Here are the tickets for you and Sirius, if you still want to come?”

“Perhaps. I’m not altogether sure if I can take that much excitement so close to my post-transformation, but I will definitely try.” Remus refrained from commenting that he wasn’t sure if Sirius would be capable of taking the excitement either once he was hexed with a rather nasty stinging jinx to his backside.

Just then Remus felt the wards shifting around the property.

Sirius was home.

“Hello, anyone in?” he called in a sing-song manner from beyond the kitchen door.

“Down here.” Remus yelled back.

“Should I leave, then?” Harry asked cautiously.

“No. I’ll talk to him about it later.”

“What’s this?” Sirius asked as he walked through the door carrying several bags of shopping and an aromatic takeaway. “A cozy little chat. Did I miss anything?” He bent down to give Remus a peck on the cheek before tousling Harry’s already disheveled hair. He collapsed into the chair next to them and looked at Remus. “Feeling alright, love?”

“Yes. How was your day?”

“Boring, mostly.” Sirius said. Then he noticed just how alert Remus was. “Have you been up long?”

“Since half two.” Remus replied.

“Really? You usually sleep until at least six. I’m sorry, Remus. If I’d known that, I would have been back sooner.”

“It’s quite alright. I enjoyed the silence, gave me plenty of time for thinking…” The scent of curry began to permeate the kitchen and Remus couldn’t help but inhale the alluring aromas. “Is that vindaloo and biryani?”

Sirius nodded. “Masala as well. From Sitara.”

Remus’s eyes lit up. “Did you get papadum?”

Sirius gave him a grin. “Of course.”

“Then I love you forever.” Remus replied, spelling the food to the table.

“I know.”

Harry snickered and rolled his eyes again.

As they were eating dinner, Harry and Sirius discussed the upcoming Harpies vs Wasps game, while Remus carefully studied them. He would nod and agree in all the right places, even comment occasionally on one of the players or strategies but couldn’t care less about the game right now. Sirius hadn’t fidgeted once in his chair during the meal, the usual sign that he was feeling guilty about something, and it unnerved him. It was quite a conundrum.

Remus knew Sirius was the culprit, so how was he able to remain as cool as a cucumber? His lover gave him a soft smile before returning to his conversation with Harry. Why does he always have to look so sweet? Remus could feel his resolve begin to crumble; it was so hard to stay mad at Sirius for any length of time. Focus, Remus! The longer he waited to do something about it, the greater the chance he would lose his nerve altogether and silently move on. Remus couldn’t give in, not this time.

After dinner they bid Harry farewell to The Burrow and began the washing up. Remus had just spelled the dishes into the sink before him when he felt Sirius’ hand leisurely rubbing his back. He melted into the gentle, loving caress of his partner and sighed contentedly.

Sirius chuckled, now moving both hands upward and massaging Remus’ neck. “You seem less tense this month.” He observed, pressing against the surprisingly supple plane of his shoulders. “I suppose that’s the new Wolfsbane then, yeh?”

“Yeah, -ah,” Remus began as Sirius kneaded a particularly tight knot that he’d found. “there’s a general… ah… improvement all around.” Sirius continued working at the few remaining knots, relaxing Remus even further. He reminisced briefly on all the selfless acts that Sirius had performed for him over the years. It was almost enough for Remus to feel guilty over his cunning plan, but not quite.

Sirius kissed the nape of his neck. “I’m glad, love.” He said genuinely, even though it meant acknowledging the reasoning behind it.

“Perhaps you could show your appreciation for Severus’ hard work, then?” Remus proposed.
